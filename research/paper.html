6. Financials & Cost Structure: The Path to Profitability

A. Detailed Infrastructure Costs

The proposed AWS stack is secure, scalable, and optimized for a SaaS model. This is not a "lean" MVP setup; it is a professional, enterprise-grade foundation that can support millions of users.

B. Revenue & Profitability Forecast

The key metric for SaaS is the **Lifetime Value (LTV) to Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** ratio. Your current numbers already show a very healthy LTV:CAC.

**LTV (Lifetime Value):** At a blended ARPU of ₹375 and a churn rate of 5%, the average customer will stay for 20 months. LTV is therefore `₹375 * 20 = ₹7,500`.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Your target is `< ₹1,000`. This means for every ₹1,000 you spend on marketing, you acquire a paying user.

**LTV:CAC Ratio:** `₹7,500 / ₹1,000 = 7.5x`. An LTV:CAC ratio of 3x or higher is considered excellent. Your ratio of 7.5x indicates an extremely profitable and sustainable business model, a very attractive metric for investors.

8. System Design: The Technical Blueprint

Our system design is built for stability, scalability, and speed.

  • Frontend (Next.js on Vercel): Chosen for its incredible performance, built-in SEO optimizations, and seamless integration with Vercel's global CDN.
  • Auth (Clerk): Provides a secure, fully-managed authentication service that adheres to modern security standards, allowing us to focus on our core product.
  • Backend (FastAPI Microservices): A high-performance Python framework. We will use a microservices architecture to break down the system into manageable, scalable parts: `blog-service`, `ai-service`, and `social-service`.
  • Database (Aurora Postgres): A fully managed, scalable, and secure relational database that can handle our growth.
  • Queues (SQS): All background tasks (like generating an AI-powered blog draft or scheduling a social post) are put on an SQS queue.
  • Security: Our security is baked in from the ground up, with Row-Level Security, MFA, and automated backups.
